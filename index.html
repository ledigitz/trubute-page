Remembering Steve.

Over a million people from all over the world have shared their memories, thoughts, and feelings about Steve. One thing they all have in common — from personal friends to colleagues to owners of Apple products — is how they’ve been touched by his passion and creativity. You can view some of these messages below.
And share your own at rememberingsteve@apple.com

Thank You

I honestly think it is because of my time on an Apple that I choose to become a graphic designer. It's truly a sad day and my thoughts and prayers are with his friends and family. Steve... You'll be missed.

good bye and thank you!

Dear Apple Team, I would like to share my thoughts with you. When I saw Steve´s speech at stanford on youtube I thought, somehow, he was also talking about me. I dropped out of college in New Jersey and that also was the best thing that ever happened to me. I didnt know what to do and my parents cut me out- I had a couple of bucks in my pocket and, since I am peruvian who went to school to play soccer, I was facing a visa status change to "ilegal " I couldn't return home because I had this feeling I wasted a lot of my parents money in a college not only I didnt finished, but I also didnt care about. So I decided to stay in the US. I moved to Virginia and started working in a restaurant. I started as a busboy and ended up as an assistant manager. I loved the restaurant life and I fell in love with the kitchen It´s been 7 years now and after working with many important chefs in the Washington DC area, I returned to Peru were I´m finishing my cuisine diploma at Le Cordon Bleu Peru and I am also opening my own restaurant at the end of this month. So I guess the dots conected somehow. From Lima, Peru Good bye and Thank you Steve Jobs!

Christian from Lima, Peru
